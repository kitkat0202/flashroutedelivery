Frequently Asked Questions

What services do you offer?

> We offer commission-based last-mile delivery services for warehouses and businesses, ensuring timely and reliable deliveries to customers.

How can I become a driver?

> If you're interested in joining our team, fill out the driver application form on our website. We’re looking for motivated drivers with their own reliable vehicle. We offer flexible schedules, commission-based earnings, and competitive rates per delivery.

What type of vehicles do I need to drive for FlashRoute Delivery?

> Drivers need to have their own vehicle, such as a sedan, SUV, or van. No company vehicles are provided.

How do you ensure timely deliveries?

> Our drivers are experienced and follow optimized routes to ensure timely deliveries. We prioritize customer satisfaction and strive for on-time performance with every delivery.

How are drivers paid?

> Drivers are paid on a commission-based structure. We offer competitive rates per delivery, with weekly payouts for all completed work.

How many deliveries am I expected to complete daily?

> Drivers are expected to complete a set amount of small parcels each day, typically around 60-130 parcels. You will start with around 60 parcels, and if you feel you can handle more, the number will increase. Your day is done only after you finish all of your deliveries.

Do I need to provide my own equipment?

> Yes, drivers are required to provide their own equipment, including a reliable vehicle for deliveries. We do not provide any equipment for drivers.

Will I receive support during my deliveries?

> Yes, while we do not provide equipment, a dispatcher will be assigned to your team. The dispatcher will monitor your progress to ensure you are completing deliveries in a timely manner and taking the correct proof of delivery photos. They will also be available to assist with any questions or concerns you may have during your route.

How flexible is the delivery schedule?

> The schedule is flexible, but drivers are expected to complete all deliveries assigned for the day. The exact hours can vary, but the day ends once all deliveries are completed. You can increase your parcel load as you become more comfortable with the routes.
